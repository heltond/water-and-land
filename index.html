About this map

This map shows comparisons between water pollution and land use in Washington. The state is divided into Water Resource Inventory Areas (WRIAs) and shaded according to the relative frequency of land use types within each WRIA. The map compares where certain land use types and water pollutants are similarly frequent or infrequent.

On the map, a darker color indicates a higher relative frequency of the associated land type, while a lighter color indicates a lower frequency. The checkbox in the top right controls which of five kinds of water pollution have been recorded. Click on any map to zoom in; click again to return to the default view.

Mapping files and data from the Washington State Department of Ecology and Natural Earth.
